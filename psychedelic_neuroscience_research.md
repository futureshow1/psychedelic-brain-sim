## 1. DEFAULT MODE NETWORK (DMN)

### Anatomical Location (for 3D visualization)
**Core nodes:**
- **Medial Prefrontal Cortex (mPFC)**: MNI approx. (1, 55, -3) -- anterior midline, Brodmann areas 10, 11, 12, 32
- **Posterior Cingulate Cortex (PCC) / Precuneus**: MNI approx. (1, -61, 38) -- posterior midline, BA 23, 31, 7
- **Angular Gyrus (bilateral)**: MNI approx. (-40, -70, 40) left; (44, -64, 32) right -- inferior parietal lobule, BA 39
- **Inferior Parietal Lobule**: lateral parietal surface

**Subsystem nodes:**
- *Dorsal Medial Subsystem* (social cognition): dmPFC, TPJ, lateral temporal cortex, anterior temporal pole
- *Medial Temporal Subsystem* (memory): hippocampus, parahippocampus, retrosplenial cortex, posterior IPL

### Normal Function
- Self-referential processing, autobiographical memory, future planning
- "Mind-wandering" and spontaneous thought
- Theory of mind / mentalizing about others
- Integration of bottom-up attention with memory and perception
- Active during rest, deactivated during externally-focused tasks

### Effects of Psychedelics

| Substance | Effect on DMN | Key Findings |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| **Psilocybin** | Strong disruption | Decreased CBF and BOLD in ACC, PCC, precuneus, angular cortex, mPFC. Decoupling of mPFC-PCC. Within-DMN FC decrease persists 3 weeks. Anterior hippocampus-DMN persistent FC decrease (Nature 2024, Siegel et al.) |
| **LSD** | Strong disruption | Significantly decreased coactivation within medial-posterior DMN. Reduced oscillatory amplitude across mPFC, PCC, bilateral temporoparietal cortex. Increased cortical entropy in DMN regions |
| **Ayahuasca/DMT** | Moderate disruption | Decreased FC within PCC. Increased connectivity within ACC. Enhanced coupling PCC to visual cortex. Long-term: cortical thinning in precuneus/PCC, thickening in ACC |
| **MDMA** | Mild disruption | Less DMN disruption than classic psychedelics; reduced connectivity but primarily affects SN/insula circuits |
| **Ketamine** | Moderate disruption | Disrupts DMN via NMDA blockade rather than 5-HT2A; 3 Hz oscillation in posteromedial cortex linked to dissociation |
| **5-MeO-DMT** | Strong disruption | Rapid ego dissolution correlating with DMN decoherence; increases gamma oscillations |
| **Mescaline** | Moderate disruption | Classic 5-HT2A mediated DMN disruption, similar pattern to psilocybin/LSD |
| **Ibogaine** | Atypical pattern | Complex pharmacology (opioid, NMDA, nAChR); limited neuroimaging data; effects likely differ from classic psychedelics |

### Subjective Experience
DMN disruption correlates with: ego dissolution, loss of self-referential thought, dissolution of narrative self, oceanic boundlessness, feelings of unity

### Key Neuroimaging Evidence
- Carhart-Harris et al., 2012 (PNAS): First fMRI demonstration of DMN deactivation under psilocybin
- Siegel et al., 2024 (Nature): Psilocybin causes >3x greater FC disruption than methylphenidate; persistent hippocampus-DMN FC decrease
- Palhano-Fontes et al., 2015 (PLOS ONE): Ayahuasca modulates DMN activity and connectivity
- Systematic review (Int J Neuropsychopharmacol, 2023): Consistent acute within-DMN disruption across all classic psychedelics

## 2. SALIENCE NETWORK (SN)

### Anatomical Location
- **Anterior Insula (AI)**: bilateral, deep to lateral sulcus, BA 13/14/16 -- MNI approx. (36, 18, 2) right; (-36, 18, 2) left
- **Dorsal Anterior Cingulate Cortex (dACC)**: midline frontal, BA 24/32 -- MNI approx. (0, 22, 35)
- **Amygdala**: medial temporal lobe, anterior to hippocampus
- **Supplementary motor area**
- **Supramarginal gyrus**

### Normal Function
- Detecting and filtering salient stimuli (both internal and external)
- Switching between DMN (internal) and CEN (external) attention
- Integrating emotional and sensory information
- Autonomic regulation, pain processing
- "Gatekeeper" function between internal and external awareness

### Effects of Psychedelics

| Substance | Effect on SN | Key Findings |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|
| **Psilocybin** | Reduced within-SN integrity | Decreased within-SN effective connectivity; reduced inter-hemispheric communication |
| **LSD** | Increased SN-DMN coupling | Enhanced between-network connectivity SN-DMN; dissolution of SN boundaries |
| **DMT** | Increased SN-DMN connectivity | Increased connectivity within ACC of SN; enhanced DMN-SN coupling |
| **MDMA** | Decreased right insula/SN FC | Decreased right insula/SN connectivity correlating with baseline anxiety and altered bodily sensations |
| **Ketamine** | Altered SN dynamics | NMDA-mediated disruption of salience detection |
| **5-MeO-DMT** | Enhanced SN disruption | Rapid dissolution of SN-mediated filtering |

### Subjective Experience
SN changes correlate with: altered significance attribution, everything feeling "meaningful," blurring of internal/external boundaries, heightened emotional salience, altered bodily sensations

### Key Evidence
- Preller et al., 2020: LSD effects on SN-DMN coupling
- Roseman et al., 2014: Psilocybin and network segregation changes
- Walpola et al., 2017: MDMA and altered insula connectivity (Neuropsychopharmacology)

## 3. EXECUTIVE CONTROL NETWORK (ECN) / Central Executive Network (CEN)

### Anatomical Location
- **Dorsolateral Prefrontal Cortex (dlPFC)**: lateral frontal lobe, BA 9/46 -- MNI approx. (-44, 36, 20) left; (44, 36, 20) right
- **Posterior Parietal Cortex (PPC)**: superior parietal lobule, BA 7/40 -- MNI approx. (-50, -52, 45) left
- **Frontal eye fields**: BA 8
- **Lateral cerebellum**

### Normal Function
- Working memory maintenance and manipulation
- Cognitive flexibility and set-shifting
- Planning, problem-solving, logical reasoning
- Top-down attentional control
- Response inhibition and conflict resolution

### Effects of Psychedelics

| Substance | Effect on ECN | Key Findings |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| **Psilocybin** | Mixed: increased within-CEN EC but reduced top-down control | Decreased top-down EC from resting-state networks to amygdala; within-CEN EC increased |
| **LSD** | Increased global CEN flexibility | Enhanced global connectivity and flexibility of CEN; associated with emotional changes |
| **DMT** | Disrupted executive control | Reduced prefrontal top-down signaling |
| **MDMA** | Enhanced prefrontal activation | Increased PFC activity alongside decreased amygdala activity |
| **Ketamine** | Enhanced gamma in PFC | Increased pyramidal neuron excitability via interneuron disinhibition; enhanced gamma oscillations |

### Subjective Experience
ECN changes correlate with: loosened cognitive control, creative/divergent thinking, inability to maintain focused attention, enhanced cognitive flexibility post-acutely, reduced mental rigidity

### Key Evidence
- Carhart-Harris & Friston, 2019 (REBUS model): Cognitive control as process that stabilizes thought
- Nature 2024 (Siegel et al.): Massive disruption of FC including frontoparietal networks
- 2025 study on dlPFC role in ego dissolution and emotional arousal during psychedelic state

## 4. VISUAL CORTEX (V1-V5)

### Anatomical Location
- **V1 (Primary visual cortex)**: calcarine sulcus, occipital pole, BA 17 -- MNI approx. (0, -84, 4)
- **V2**: surrounding V1, BA 18 -- MNI approx. (0, -78, 12)
- **V3**: anterior to V2, BA 19
- **V4 (Color/form)**: fusiform gyrus/ventral occipital -- MNI approx. (-30, -72, -12)
- **V5/MT (Motion)**: lateral occipitotemporal -- MNI approx. (-44, -68, 0)
- **Lateral Occipital Complex (LOC)**: lateral occipital surface

### Normal Function
- V1: Edge detection, orientation, spatial frequency (primary processing)
- V2: Figure-ground segregation, illusory contours, texture processing
- V3: Global motion, dynamic form
- V4: Color perception, form recognition, attentional modulation
- V5/MT: Motion perception, speed detection

### Effects of Psychedelics on Visual Cortex

**Mechanism of visual hallucinations:**
1. 5-HT2A receptors are highly expressed throughout visual cortex
2. Activation of 5-HT2A reduces visually-evoked (but not spontaneous) activity in V1
3. This creates a state where spontaneous neuronal excitation overwhelms stimulus-driven cortical excitation
4. Modulation of alpha oscillations shifts processing mode
5. Decreased N170 potentials in LOC and V2 lead to incoherent object representation and arbitrary filling-in

| Substance | Effect on Visual Cortex | Key Findings |
|-----------|------------------------|--------------|
| **Psilocybin** | Disrupted modal object completion | Decreased N170 VEPs; reduced stimulus-driven V1 response; increased spontaneous activity |
| **LSD** | Massive visual cortex activation | Robust increases in V1-V3 FC with rest of brain; visual cortex "talks to" regions it normally doesn't |
| **DMT** | Enhanced visual processing | Increased connectivity from primary and extrastriate cortices; "waking dream" state with eyes closed |
| **Ayahuasca** | Enhanced visual pathway coupling | Enhanced FC in third visual pathway; increased visual cortex to PCC coupling |
| **Mescaline** | Strong visual effects | Geometric patterns likely via V1/V2 interference with orientation-selective neurons |
| **5-MeO-DMT** | Less visual, more somatic | Less visual cortex activation compared to DMT; more dominated by somatic/ego-dissolution effects |

### Subjective Experience
Visual cortex changes correlate with: geometric patterns (V1/V2 Turing-type instabilities), enhanced color saturation (V4), visual trailing/tracers (V5), complex hallucinations (higher visual areas + top-down filling-in), closed-eye visions

### Key Evidence
- Kometer et al., 2013 (J Neurosci): Psilocybin effects on alpha oscillations and N170 VEPs blocked by 5-HT2A antagonist ketanserin
- Michaiel et al., 2019 (Cell Reports): DOI reduces visual response gain and alters temporal dynamics in mouse V1
- Carhart-Harris et al., 2016 (PNAS): LSD multimodal neuroimaging showing visual cortex hyperconnectivity

## 5. THALAMUS

### Anatomical Location
- Bilateral diencephalic structure, deep central brain
- MNI approx. center: (0, -12, 8) -- sits atop brainstem
- Surrounds third ventricle

**Key nuclei for psychedelic research:**
- **Mediodorsal (MD)**: connects to PFC -- MNI approx. (6, -16, 8)
- **Pulvinar (medial PuM, anterior PuA)**: connects to visual/association cortex -- posterior thalamus
- **Ventral Anterior (VA)**: motor circuits
- **Ventral Lateral (VL)**: motor circuits, cerebellar input
- **Ventral Posterior (VP)**: somatosensory relay
- **Lateral Dorsal/Lateral Posterior (LD/LP)**: spatial orientation, memory
- **Reticular nucleus**: thin shell surrounding thalamus, GABAergic gate

### Normal Function
- Sensory relay station (all senses except olfaction)
- Thalamic gating: filters and regulates information flow to cortex
- Cortico-striato-thalamo-cortical (CSTC) loops: feedback circuits controlling consciousness
- Reticular nucleus provides inhibitory gating
- Arousal regulation, attention, consciousness

### Vollenweider's Thalamic Gating Theory (CSTC Model)
The CSTC model proposes:
1. Thalamus normally gates/filters sensory information before cortical projection
2. Feedback loops between cortex, striatum, and thalamus regulate this filtering
3. Psychedelics (via 5-HT2A on cortical pyramidal neurons) disrupt these loops
4. Result: thalamic gate "opens" -- sensory flooding of cortex
5. This causes hallucinations, cognitive disruption, ego dissolution

### Effects of Psychedelics

| Substance | Effect on Thalamus | Key Findings |
|-----------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Psilocybin** | Localized to MD and pulvinar nuclei | TIC05 (MD) p=0.01 decreased; TIC03 (PuM) p=0.006 decreased; decreased thalamocortical connectivity to DMN and visual networks |
| **LSD** | Increased thalamo-cortical connectivity | Increased effective connectivity from thalamus to cortex via 5-HT2A; decreased effective connectivity from ventral striatum to thalamus independently of 5-HT2A |
| **DMT** | Thalamic gate opening | Increased thalamic relay to cortex consistent with CSTC model |
| **Ketamine** | Disrupted thalamocortical communication | NMDA blockade on thalamic interneurons; desynchronized oscillatory activity |
| **MDMA** | Mild thalamic modulation | Less direct thalamic gating disruption than classic psychedelics |

### Subjective Experience
Thalamic changes correlate with: sensory flooding, synesthesia-like experiences, inability to filter stimuli, mystical experience scores (smaller TIC07 decrease = higher mystical scores, r=0.54), "letting go" (larger TIC03 decrease, r=-0.47)

### Key Evidence
- Vollenweider & Geyer: Original CSTC model
- Muller et al., 2019 (PNAS): LSD effective connectivity changes in thalamo-cortical circuits
- Preller et al., 2022 (NeuroImage): Psilocybin effects localized to mediodorsal and pulvinar nuclei
- Avram et al., 2023 (NeuroImage): LSD-induced changes in distinct thalamic nuclei FC

## 6. AMYGDALA

### Anatomical Location
- Bilateral, medial temporal lobe, anterior to hippocampus
- MNI approx. (-24, -4, -18) left; (24, -4, -18) right
- Subdivisions: basolateral (BLA), central (CeA), medial nuclei
- Deep within temporal lobe, almond-shaped

### Normal Function
- Fear conditioning and fear response
- Emotional salience detection
- Threat processing and fight-or-flight
- Emotional memory consolidation
- Social evaluation and facial emotion recognition

### Effects of Psychedelics

| Substance | Effect on Amygdala | Key Findings |
|-----------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Psilocybin** | Acutely decreased to negative stimuli; POST-treatment INCREASED to all emotions | Acute: weakened amygdala-to-V1 modulatory effect of visual threat. Post-treatment (depression): increased amygdala responses to fearful AND happy faces -- opposite of SSRIs |
| **LSD** | Decreased reactivity to fearful faces | Amygdala deactivation correlated with subjective psychedelic effects (negative correlation, r significant) |
| **Ayahuasca** | Decreased activity to negative stimuli | Reduced amygdala response to threatening stimuli |
| **MDMA** | Decreased amygdala activity | Oxytocin-mediated; decreased amygdala with increased PFC activation; enhanced fear extinction |
| **DMT** | Altered amygdala connectivity | Increased coupling from ACC to right limbic structures including amygdala |
| **Ketamine** | Reduced amygdala reactivity | Blunted emotional reactivity via NMDA/glutamate mechanisms |

### Subjective Experience
Amygdala changes correlate with: reduced fear/anxiety acutely, emotional openness, ability to confront difficult emotions (therapeutic window), post-treatment emotional reconnection (vs. SSRI emotional blunting)

### Key Evidence
- Mueller et al., 2017 (Transl Psychiatry): LSD decreased amygdala reactivity to fearful stimuli
- Mertens et al., 2020 (J Psychopharmacol): Psilocybin changes amygdala-PFC connectivity in treatment-resistant depression
- Roseman et al., 2018 (Neuropharmacology): Increased amygdala response post-psilocybin treatment
- Carhart-Harris et al., 2017 (Sci Rep): Psilocybin for TRD, amygdala-PFC changes

## 7. HIPPOCAMPUS

### Anatomical Location
- Bilateral medial temporal lobe, curved structure
- MNI approx. (-26, -20, -14) left; (26, -20, -14) right
- **Anterior hippocampus** (head): emotional memory, novelty detection
- **Posterior hippocampus** (tail): spatial memory, navigation
- **Dentate gyrus (DG)**: subgranular zone -- key neurogenesis site
- Includes CA1, CA3, DG subfields

### Normal Function
- Episodic memory encoding and retrieval
- Spatial navigation and cognitive maps
- Emotional memory consolidation
- Pattern separation (DG) and pattern completion (CA3)
- Adult neurogenesis in dentate gyrus subgranular zone

### Effects of Psychedelics

| Substance | Effect on Hippocampus | Key Findings |
|-----------|----------------------|--------------|
| **Psilocybin** | Persistent anterior hippocampus-DMN FC decrease | FC decrease lasting 3+ weeks (Nature 2024). Slightly reduced neurogenesis in mice. BDNF-TrkB and mTOR pathway activation |
| **LSD** | Altered hippocampal connectivity | Increased BDNF plasma levels at 4h and 6h. Enhanced synaptic plasticity |
| **DMT** | Enhanced neurogenesis | Activated subgranular zone of DG; promoted new neurons, astrocytes, oligodendrocytes; improved memory in mice. Sigma-1 receptor mediated |
| **5-MeO-DMT** | Increased neurogenesis | Significantly increased cell proliferation in DG; more complex dendritic trees in granule cells |
| **Ayahuasca** | Structural changes | Chronic use: cortical thickening in hippocampal regions. DMT component drives neurogenesis |
| **MDMA** | Enhanced hippocampus-vmPFC connectivity | Increased connectivity supporting fear extinction and memory reconsolidation |
| **Ketamine** | Suppressed cortico-hippocampal drive | Reduced top-down mPFC-to-hippocampus connectivity; enhanced AMPAR function |
| **Ibogaine** | Unknown in humans | No published neuroimaging; animal data suggests hippocampal sigma-1 involvement |

### Subjective Experience
Hippocampal changes correlate with: altered sense of time and space, vivid autobiographical memories, feeling of timelessness, deja vu, novel associations between memories, sense of "newness"

### Key Evidence
- Siegel et al., 2024 (Nature): Persistent hippocampus-DMN FC decrease = potential correlate of proplasticity
- Morales-Garcia et al., 2020 (Transl Psychiatry): DMT neurogenesis in vivo and in vitro
- Lima da Cruz et al., 2018 (Front Mol Neurosci): 5-MeO-DMT cell proliferation in ventral DG

## 8. CLAUSTRUM

### Anatomical Location
- Thin, irregular sheet of grey matter
- Bilateral, deep to insular cortex, between insula and putamen
- MNI approx. (-30, 16, 2) left; (30, 16, 2) right
- One of the most densely connected structures in the brain
- Extremely high density of 5-HT2A receptors

### Normal Function (Crick's "Seat of Consciousness" hypothesis)
- Francis Crick proposed claustrum as key to unified conscious experience
- Coordinates and synchronizes activity across cortical areas
- Binds multisensory information into coherent percepts
- Orchestrates attention across modalities
- Proposed "conductor of consciousness" -- integrating diverse brain regions

### Effects of Psychedelics

| Substance | Effect on Claustrum | Key Findings |
|-----------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Psilocybin** | 15-30% activity reduction | Reduced ALFF and signal variance bilaterally. R claustrum-DMN FC decreased. R claustrum-auditory FC decreased. L claustrum-FPTC FC decreased. BUT R claustrum-FPTC FC increased |
| **LSD** | Disrupted claustral coordination | Reduced prefrontal communication through claustrum |
| **DMT** | Likely disrupted | High 5-HT2A density suggests strong effects; limited direct data |

### The Cortico-Claustro-Cortical (CCC) Model
Proposes psychedelic action via modulation of claustral mechanisms through 5-HT2A receptor agonism, disrupting the claustrum's role as a cortical integrator.

### 2025 Preprint (bioRxiv)
Using spectral dynamic causal modeling: psilocybin enhanced claustral INHIBITION of cortical networks while DISINHIBITING subcortical areas, partially associated with psychedelic subjective effect scores.

### Subjective Experience
Claustrum changes correlate with: ego dissolution, mystical experience ratings, ineffability, fragmentation of unified consciousness, altered sense of cohesion

### Key Evidence
- Barrett et al., 2020 (NeuroImage): Johns Hopkins study, psilocybin reduces claustrum activity 15-30%
- Crick & Koch, 2005: Original claustrum consciousness hypothesis
- 2025 bioRxiv preprint: Effective connectivity of claustrum under psilocybin

## 9. PREFRONTAL CORTEX (Subdivisions)

### Anatomical Location
**Three key subdivisions:**
- **Medial PFC (mPFC)**: midline frontal, BA 10/11/12/32 -- MNI approx. (0, 52, 8)
  - Ventromedial PFC (vmPFC): BA 11/12, orbital surface
  - Dorsomedial PFC (dmPFC): BA 8/9, superior medial surface
- **Lateral PFC / Dorsolateral PFC (dlPFC)**: lateral frontal, BA 9/46 -- MNI approx. (44, 36, 20)
- **Orbitofrontal Cortex (OFC)**: ventral frontal surface, BA 11/47 -- MNI approx. (30, 36, -12)

### Normal Function
- **mPFC**: Self-referential processing, emotional evaluation, social cognition, autobiographical memory
- **dlPFC**: Working memory, cognitive control, planning, response inhibition
- **OFC**: Reward valuation, decision-making, impulse control, emotional regulation

### Effects of Psychedelics

| Subdivision | Effect Under Psychedelics | Key Findings |
|-------------|--------------------------|--------------|
| **mPFC** | Consistently deactivated | Magnitude of deactivation predicts intensity of subjective effects (Carhart-Harris 2012). Key DMN hub disruption |
| **dlPFC** | Reduced top-down control acutely | Associated with ego dissolution and emotional arousal. Post-treatment: increased response to emotionally conflicting stimuli |
| **vmPFC** | Decreased FC with amygdala | Particularly for fearful/neutral faces; therapeutic relevance for PTSD/depression |
| **OFC** | Decreased activity | Lateral OFC shows significant CBF decreases under psilocybin |
| **dmPFC** | Disrupted social processing | Part of DMN dorsal medial subsystem; altered theory of mind |

### Subjective Experience
PFC changes correlate with: loosened executive control, reduced self-criticism, emotional breakthrough, creative thinking, reduced rumination, altered decision-making and value judgments

### Key Evidence
- Carhart-Harris et al., 2012 (PNAS): mPFC deactivation magnitude predicts subjective effects
- 2025 study (Human Brain Mapping): dlPFC role in ego dissolution
- Mertens et al., 2020: vmPFC-amygdala connectivity changes in depression treatment

## 10. RAPHE NUCLEI

### Anatomical Location
- **Dorsal Raphe Nucleus (DRN)**: midbrain/pons, periaqueductal grey, midline -- MNI approx. (0, -28, -18)
- **Median Raphe Nucleus (MRN)**: midline pons, ventral to DRN -- MNI approx. (0, -32, -24)
- Both in brainstem tegmentum
- Contains diverse neuronal populations: serotonergic, glutamatergic, GABAergic, dopaminergic

### Normal Function
- Primary source of serotonin (5-HT) for the entire brain
- DRN: ~50% of brain's serotonin neurons; projects broadly to cortex, thalamus, basal ganglia, limbic system
- MRN: projects to hippocampus, septum
- Mood regulation, sleep-wake cycle, appetite, pain modulation
- Regulate behavioral states and arousal

### Effects of Psychedelics

| Substance | Effect on Raphe Nuclei | Key Findings |
|-----------|----------------------|--------------|
| **LSD** | Near-complete cessation of firing | DRN serotonergic neurons stop firing within 1-2 minutes. Mediated by 5-HT1A autoreceptors on somatodendritic region |
| **Psilocybin/psilocin** | Suppressed serotonergic firing | Via 5-HT1A agonism at raphe; reduces serotonin output while 5-HT2A cortical effects dominate |
| **DMT** | Reduced raphe firing | Similar 5-HT1A autoreceptor mechanism |

**Critical point**: The cessation of raphe firing means the psychedelic state features REDUCED endogenous serotonin release while exogenous agonism of cortical 5-HT2A receptors drives the subjective effects. This paradox is key to understanding the mechanism.

### Subjective Experience
Raphe changes correlate with: the overall shift in serotonergic tone from brainstem-regulated homeostasis to cortex-dominated psychedelic processing

### Key Evidence
- Aghajanian & Marek, 1999 (Neuropsychopharmacology): Classic work on raphe suppression
- Aghajanian, 1972: Original DRN cessation findings with LSD

## 11. LOCUS COERULEUS (LC)

### Anatomical Location
- Bilateral, dorsal pons, lateral to fourth ventricle floor
- MNI approx. (-2, -36, -22) to (2, -36, -22) -- very small nucleus
- ~15,000-30,000 neurons per side (in humans)
- Dense noradrenergic nucleus; highly pigmented (blue-black)

### Normal Function
- Primary source of norepinephrine (NE) for entire brain
- Arousal, wakefulness, alertness
- Attentional focusing (phasic) vs. exploration (tonic) modes
- Stress response modulation
- Memory consolidation (especially emotional)
- Gain modulation of cortical processing

### Effects of Psychedelics

| Substance | Effect on LC | Key Findings |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| **LSD** | Decreased spontaneous, increased evoked activity | Net effect: enhanced signal-to-noise of sensory responses via 5-HT2A on LC afferents |
| **Mescaline/DOM** | Similar dual effect | Decreased spontaneous + increased evoked activity; blocked by 5-HT2 antagonists |
| **Psilocybin** | Indirect modulation | Via 5-HT2A receptors on LC-projecting neurons |
| **MDMA** | Massive NE release | Direct norepinephrine transporter reversal; contributes to arousal and physiological activation |
| **Ketamine** | Enhanced NE signaling | Indirect via NMDA blockade |

### Subjective Experience
LC changes correlate with: heightened arousal, enhanced sensory sensitivity, pupil dilation, sense of hyper-alertness, physiological activation (sweating, heart rate)

### Key Evidence
- Aghajanian, 1980: Mescaline/LSD effects on LC neurons
- Classic electrophysiology studies showing 5-HT2A mediation of LC responses

## 12. ANTERIOR CINGULATE CORTEX (ACC)

### Anatomical Location
- Midline frontal, surrounding corpus callosum anteriorly
- **Dorsal ACC (dACC)**: BA 24/32, MNI approx. (0, 22, 35) -- cognitive division
- **Subgenual ACC (sgACC)**: BA 25, MNI approx. (0, 25, -10) -- emotional/affective division
- **Pregenual ACC**: BA 32, transitional zone

### Normal Function
- **dACC**: Error detection, conflict monitoring, performance monitoring, cognitive control
- **sgACC**: Emotional regulation, mood, autonomic visceral functions (linked to depression)
- Pain processing (both physical and social)
- Decision-making under uncertainty
- Integration of emotional and cognitive processing

### Effects of Psychedelics

| Substance | Effect on ACC | Key Findings |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| **Psilocybin** | Decreased CBF and BOLD | Consistent deactivation predicting subjective effects. LFP: decreased low-frequency power, increased population firing rate (rodent data). Post-treatment: increased dACC/mOFC response to emotional conflict |
| **LSD** | Deactivated; increased entropy | Reduced oscillatory amplitude; enhanced connectivity to DMN |
| **DMT/Ayahuasca** | Increased connectivity within ACC | Increased coupling from ACC to hippocampus, parahippocampal gyrus, amygdala |
| **MDMA** | Altered ACC processing | Changes in emotional conflict processing |
| **Ketamine** | Modulated via glutamate | NMDA blockade affects ACC error signaling |

### Subjective Experience
ACC changes correlate with: reduced self-criticism, diminished "inner critic," altered error monitoring, emotional acceptance, reduced rumination, changed relationship with negative thoughts

### Key Evidence
- Carhart-Harris et al., 2012: ACC deactivation as predictor of psilocybin intensity
- Preller et al., 2020: ACC connectivity changes under LSD
- Shao et al., 2022 (Sci Rep): Psilocybin reduces LFP power in ACC

## 13. POSTERIOR CINGULATE CORTEX (PCC)

### Anatomical Location
- Midline parietal, posterior to corpus callosum
- BA 23/31 -- MNI approx. (0, -52, 26)
- Continuous with precuneus dorsally and retrosplenial cortex ventrally
- Major DMN hub; one of the most metabolically active brain regions at rest

### Normal Function
- Self-referential processing (core sense of "I")
- Autobiographical memory retrieval
- Internal mental simulation
- Mind-wandering and internally-directed thought
- Integration of memory with spatial/self information
- "Egocentric" spatial representation

### Effects of Psychedelics

| Substance | Effect on PCC | Key Findings |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| **Psilocybin** | Strong deactivation | Decreased CBF, BOLD signal. Decreased alpha power correlating with ego dissolution. FC decrease with anterior hippocampus persisting weeks |
| **LSD** | Strong deactivation | Decreased activation and within-DMN connectivity; increased global entropy |
| **Ayahuasca** | Decreased FC within PCC | But increased coupling PCC-to-ACC and PCC-to-visual cortex. Long-term: cortical thinning |
| **DMT** | Increased PCC-to-ACC coupling | Via fMRI: increased connectivity from PCC to ACC and visual cortex |
| **Ketamine** | 3 Hz oscillation | Posteromedial cortex (including PCC) shows characteristic dissociative rhythm |
| **5-MeO-DMT** | Rapid PCC disruption | Associated with fast-onset ego dissolution |

### Subjective Experience
PCC changes are THE strongest correlate of ego dissolution. Decreased PCC alpha power = strongest predictor of ego disintegration. Loss of self-referential narrative, dissolution of autobiographical self, oceanic boundlessness.

### Key Evidence
- Carhart-Harris et al., 2012: PCC as key node deactivated by psilocybin
- Muthukumaraswamy et al., 2013: PCC alpha desynchronization correlating with ego dissolution
- Li et al., 2023 (Nature Comms): Ketamine 3 Hz rhythm in posteromedial cortex

## 14. TEMPORAL-PARIETAL JUNCTION (TPJ)

### Anatomical Location
- Bilateral, junction of temporal and parietal lobes
- Includes angular gyrus (BA 39) and posterior superior temporal sulcus (pSTS)
- MNI approx. (-50, -56, 26) left; (50, -56, 26) right
- Overlaps with inferior parietal lobule

### Normal Function
- Body ownership and embodied self-awareness
- Distinguishing self from other
- Theory of mind / perspective-taking
- Multisensory integration (visual, vestibular, proprioceptive)
- Spatial attention and reorienting
- Out-of-body experience site when disrupted

### Effects of Psychedelics

| Substance | Effect on TPJ | Key Findings |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| **LSD** | Increased global FC | Degree of ego dissolution correlated with bilateral TPJ FC changes (angular gyrus specifically) |

| **DMT** | Increased right pSTS connectivity | Highest dose increased right posterior STS connectivity and visual-to-mirror neuron modulation |
| **5-MeO-DMT** | Disrupted body schema | Rapid depersonalization suggesting strong TPJ effects |

### Subjective Experience
TPJ changes correlate with: out-of-body experiences, altered body boundaries, feeling of "floating," merging with environment, loss of distinction between self and world, depersonalization, altered sense of physical embodiment

### Key Evidence
- Blanke & Arzy, 2005: TPJ as neural substrate of OBEs
- Tagliazucchi et al., 2016: LSD ego dissolution correlated with TPJ FC
- 2025 medRxiv preprint: Psilocybin modulates TPJ effective connectivity during OBEs

## 15. INSULA

### Anatomical Location
- Bilateral, deep within lateral sulcus (Sylvian fissure)
- Hidden beneath temporal, parietal, and frontal opercula
- **Anterior Insula (AI)**: BA 13/14/16 -- MNI approx. (-36, 18, 2) left; (36, 18, 2) right
  - Agranular cortex; connected to frontal, limbic
- **Posterior Insula (PI)**: MNI approx. (-38, -14, 6) left; (38, -14, 6) right
  - Granular cortex; connected to somatosensory

### Normal Function
- **Anterior insula**: Interoceptive awareness ("how you feel now"), emotional awareness, subjective feeling states, empathy, time perception, autonomic regulation
- **Posterior insula**: Primary interoceptive cortex, pain, temperature, visceral sensation, vestibular processing
- Integration of bodily states with emotional/cognitive processing
- Key hub connecting bottom-up interoceptive signals with top-down predictions
- Craig's model: anterior insula generates moment-to-moment subjective awareness

### Effects of Psychedelics

| Substance | Effect on Insula | Key Findings |
|-----------|-----------------|--------------|
| **Psilocybin** | Part of SN disruption | Disrupted anterior insula connectivity; altered interoceptive prediction |
| **LSD** | Enhanced interoceptive processing | Increased global insula connectivity; altered body awareness |
| **MDMA** | Decreased R insula/SN FC | Correlates with baseline anxiety and altered bodily sensations. Decreased identification of negative emotional faces |
| **DMT** | Altered insula connectivity | Changes in interoceptive signaling |
| **Ketamine** | Disrupted insular processing | Altered interoceptive prediction via glutamate mechanisms |

### Subjective Experience
Insula changes correlate with: altered body sensations (warmth, tingling, dissolution), changed emotional awareness, altered sense of physical being, empathogenic effects (especially MDMA), changed perception of internal states, time distortion

### Key Evidence
- Walpola et al., 2017 (Neuropsychopharmacology): MDMA altered insula connectivity
- Craig, 2009 (Nature Reviews Neuroscience): Anterior insula role in subjective awareness
- 2025 medRxiv: Psilocybin OBE study showing R anterior insula to R TPJ connectivity changes

# PART II: FUNCTIONAL CONNECTIVITY & THEORETICAL FRAMEWORKS

---

## A. Connectivity Changes Under Psychedelics (Summary)

### The Core Pattern (replicated across substances and studies):

**WITHIN-network connectivity: DECREASED**
- DMN within-network FC: significantly decreased
- Visual network within-network FC: significantly decreased
- Ventral attention network: significantly decreased
- Salience network: reduced integrity

**BETWEEN-network connectivity: INCREASED**
- DMN-SN coupling: increased
- DMN-CEN coupling: increased
- DMN-visual coupling: increased
- SN-CEN coupling: altered
- Global: dissolution of modular network boundaries

**Quantitative benchmark (Nature 2024):**
- Psilocybin 25mg: >3-fold greater FC change than methylphenidate 40mg
- Of 35,778 possible connections analyzed, 695 significantly changed from baseline
- Psilocin blood levels correlated with FC changes (R2 = 0.73)

### Substance-Specific Connectivity Profiles:

**Classic serotonergics (psilocybin, LSD, DMT, mescaline):**
- Strong 5-HT2A-mediated cortical effects
- DMN disruption as primary signature
- Thalamic gating disruption via CSTC loops
- Visual cortex hyperconnectivity (especially LSD)

**MDMA:**
- Primarily monoamine release (5-HT >> DA > NE) + oxytocin
- Less DMN disruption; more amygdala-PFC modulation
- Insula/SN changes dominate
- Enhanced hippocampus-vmPFC connectivity (fear extinction)

**Ketamine:**
- NMDA blockade on interneurons -> pyramidal disinhibition
- Enhanced gamma oscillations in PFC and hippocampus
- Suppressed cortico-hippocampal drive
- 3 Hz dissociative rhythm in posteromedial cortex
- DMN disruption via different mechanism than serotonergics

## B. The Entropic Brain Hypothesis (Carhart-Harris, 2014; revised 2018)

### Core Proposition
The entropy of spontaneous brain activity indexes the richness of conscious states. Psychedelics INCREASE brain entropy, moving the system toward a more disordered but informationally richer state.

### Key Concepts:
1. **Primary consciousness** (higher entropy): more flexible, less predictable, dream-like
2. **Secondary consciousness** (lower entropy): more structured, predictable, ego-bound
3. Psychedelics induce regression to primary consciousness via entropy increase
4. The variety of conscious states lies on a spectrum indexed by entropy
5. Too much entropy = psychosis/confusion; too little = rigid/depressed states

### Neurophysiological Signatures:
- Increased Lempel-Ziv complexity
- Increased sample entropy of BOLD signal
- Decreased modularity/segregation of networks
- Increased signal diversity across cortex
- Decreased alpha power (especially PCC)

---

## C. REBUS Model (Relaxed Beliefs Under Psychedelics) -- Carhart-Harris & Friston, 2019

### Core Proposition
Integrates free-energy principle with entropic brain hypothesis:
- Psychedelics RELAX the precision-weighting of high-level priors/beliefs
- This LIBERATES bottom-up information flow
- Results in unconstrained cognition and perception

### Mechanistic Steps:
1. 5-HT2A agonism on layer V pyramidal neurons in association cortex
2. Increased spontaneous cortical excitability and entropy
3. Weakened top-down predictive signaling (high-level priors lose precision)
4. Enhanced bottom-up sensory/interoceptive information flow
5. Dissolution of ingrained patterns of thought and perception
6. "Anarchic brain" state where belief hierarchies flatten

---

## D. Energy Landscape Flattening

### Core Findings (Singleton et al., 2022, Nature Communications):
Using receptor-informed network control theory:
- LSD and psilocybin REDUCE the control energy required for brain state transitions
- This = "flattening" of the brain's energy landscape
- Across individuals: reduced control energy correlates with more frequent state transitions and increased entropy of brain dynamics

### Mechanism:
- Normal brain: deep energy "wells" (attractor states) corresponding to stable network configurations
- Under psychedelics: wells become shallower, barriers between states lower
- Brain can transition more freely between diverse states
- Analogous to simulated annealing: "heating" the system increases plasticity
- As drug metabolizes and system "cools," new attractor basins form (therapeutic rewriting)

### 5-HT2A Receptor Role:
Network control analysis incorporating 5-HT2A receptor PET distribution demonstrates specific association between receptor density and control energy reduction, establishing the mechanistic link.

## E. Neural Correlates of Specific Subjective Experiences

### Ego Dissolution
- **Primary correlates**: Decreased PCC alpha power; mPFC-PCC decoupling; DMN disintegration
- **Secondary correlates**: Increased bilateral TPJ FC; decreased anterior parahippocampal-DMN FC; decreased SN integrity; reduced inter-hemispheric communication
- **Key mechanism**: Loss of coordinated self-referential network activity
- **Regions**: PCC, mPFC, TPJ, anterior hippocampus, claustrum

### Synesthesia (Cross-Modal Binding)
- **Primary mechanism**: 5-HT2A activation on layer V pyramidal cells that bind multisensory information
- **Supporting mechanism**: Reduced GABAergic inhibition allowing cross-modal activation
- **Key regions**: V1/V2 (visual), auditory cortex, parietal cortex (hyperbinding), thalamus (sensory gating failure)
- **Pathway**: Feedback loops synchronizing oscillatory responses across sensory areas; thalamic nuclei failing to discriminate sensory channels

### Mystical Experience
- **Key correlates**: Reduced claustrum activity (15-30%), decreased mPFC activity, 5-HT2A binding predicting temporal/mystical features
- **Regions**: Claustrum, mPFC, PCC, DMN as whole
- **Mechanism**: Dissolution of self-other boundaries + emotional/meaning circuits activation

### Time Distortion
- **Key correlates**: Altered anterior insula processing (Craig's model: insula generates subjective time), hippocampal disruption (temporal ordering), PFC changes (prospective timing)
- **Regions**: Anterior insula, hippocampus, PFC, cerebellum
- **5-HT2A binding**: Brain 5-HT2A receptor binding predicts temporal and mystical features (Stenbaek et al., 2021)

### Out-of-Body Experiences
- **Primary correlate**: TPJ disruption (bilateral)
- **Key mechanism**: Failure to integrate visual, vestibular, proprioceptive signals
- **Specific finding**: Psilocybin reduced R anterior insula to R TPJ effective connectivity in high-OBE participants
- **Regions**: TPJ, anterior insula, vestibular cortex

# PART III: RECEPTOR PHARMACOLOGY & SUBSTANCE COMPARISON

---

## Major Psychedelic Substances -- Receptor Profiles & Brain Effects

### 1. PSILOCYBIN / PSILOCIN
- **Primary receptor**: 5-HT2A agonist (psilocin is active metabolite)
- **Secondary**: 5-HT2C, 5-HT1A
- **Duration**: 4-6 hours
- **Most affected regions**: PCC, mPFC, claustrum, thalamus (MD, pulvinar), anterior hippocampus
- **Signature**: Strong DMN disruption, persistent hippocampus-DMN FC decrease

### 2. LSD (Lysergic Acid Diethylamide)
- **Primary receptor**: 5-HT2A agonist (very high affinity, slow dissociation)
- **Secondary**: 5-HT2C, 5-HT1A, D1, D2 dopamine receptors
- **Duration**: 8-12 hours
- **Most affected regions**: Thalamus (increased thalamocortical EC), visual cortex (massive hyperconnectivity), PCC, mPFC
- **Signature**: Strongest visual cortex hyperconnectivity; increased thalamo-cortical effective connectivity

### 3. DMT (N,N-Dimethyltryptamine)
- **Primary receptor**: 5-HT2A agonist
- **Secondary**: 5-HT1A, 5-HT2C, sigma-1 receptor (high affinity), trace amine receptors
- **Duration**: 15-30 min (smoked/IV); 4+ hours (oral as ayahuasca with MAOIs)
- **Most affected regions**: Visual cortex, ACC, hippocampus (neurogenesis via sigma-1)
- **Signature**: "Waking dream" state; sigma-1 mediated neurogenesis; action on transmodal association cortex

### 4. AYAHUASCA (DMT + MAO inhibitors)
- **Primary receptor**: Same as DMT but prolonged by MAO inhibition
- **Additional**: Harmine/harmaline (MAOIs) have own neurological effects
- **Duration**: 4-6 hours
- **Most affected regions**: PCC, mPFC, visual cortex (3rd visual pathway), mirror neuron network
- **Signature**: Enhanced 3rd visual pathway and mirror neuron connectivity; long-term cortical changes with chronic use

### 5. MESCALINE (3,4,5-trimethoxyphenethylamine)
- **Primary receptor**: 5-HT2A agonist
- **Secondary**: 5-HT2C, 5-HT2B
- **Duration**: 8-12 hours
- **Most affected regions**: Visual cortex, PFC, locus coeruleus
- **Signature**: Classic 5-HT2A pattern; strong visual geometric effects (V1/V2)

### 6. 5-MeO-DMT (5-methoxy-N,N-dimethyltryptamine)
- **Primary receptor**: 5-HT1A and 5-HT2A agonist (notable 5-HT1A preference vs. DMT)
- **Secondary**: 5-HT7, sigma-1
- **Duration**: 15-45 min (smoked/insufflated)
- **Most affected regions**: DMN (rapid dissolution), hippocampus DG (neurogenesis)
- **Signature**: Rapid, intense ego dissolution; less visual than DMT; more somatic; gamma oscillations; ventral DG neurogenesis

### 7. MDMA (3,4-methylenedioxymethamphetamine)
- **Primary mechanism**: Monoamine transporter reversal (SERT >> DAT > NET); NOT a direct 5-HT2A agonist
- **Secondary**: Oxytocin release, cortisol, BDNF
- **Duration**: 3-5 hours
- **Most affected regions**: Amygdala (decreased), insula (decreased R SN FC), PFC (increased), hippocampus-vmPFC (enhanced)
- **Signature**: Amygdala dampening + PFC enhancement = therapeutic window for trauma processing; oxytocin-mediated prosocial effects

### 8. KETAMINE (and analogs)
- **Primary mechanism**: NMDA receptor antagonist (ion channel blocker)
- **Secondary**: Opioid receptors, D2, HCN channels, AMPAR upregulation
- **Duration**: 45-90 min (IV/IM)
- **Most affected regions**: Posteromedial cortex (3 Hz rhythm), PFC (gamma increase), hippocampus (suppressed input)
- **Signature**: Dissociative 3 Hz oscillation; PV interneuron disinhibition -> pyramidal hyperexcitability; rapid AMPAR/BDNF-mediated antidepressant effect

# PART V: FOUR THEORETICAL CIRCUIT MODELS

---

## 1. CSTC Model (Vollenweider & Geyer)
**Cortico-Striato-Thalamo-Cortical loops**
- Psychedelics disrupt thalamic gating via 5-HT2A on cortical pyramidal neurons
- Reduced striatal inhibition of thalamus -> open gate -> cortical flooding
- Explains: sensory flooding, hallucinations, cognitive disruption

## 2. REBUS Model (Carhart-Harris & Friston)
**Relaxed Beliefs Under Psychedelics**
- 5-HT2A entropic effect relaxes precision of high-level priors
- Liberates bottom-up information flow
- Explains: novel perceptions, creative insight, therapeutic belief revision

## 3. CCC Model (Claustrum-based)
**Cortico-Claustro-Cortical**
- Psychedelics disrupt claustral orchestration via dense 5-HT2A receptors
- Loss of cross-cortical integration and synchronization
- Explains: fragmented consciousness, ego dissolution, altered binding

## 4. Strong Prior / Circular Inference Model
- Psychedelics amplify top-down predictions while reducing sensory precision
- Aberrant integration creates hallucinations and synesthesia
- Alternate to REBUS; debated in literature
